Profile

Polymath with five decades of experience across painting, engineering, anatomy, hydraulics, and natural philosophy. Known for a restless curiosity that unifies art and science, with a portfolio spanning altarpieces, flying machines, urban infrastructure, and the most studied portrait in human history. Fluent in observation; equally comfortable with a quill and a chisel.

Experience
First Painter, Engineer & Architect to the King 1516 – 1519
Court of Francis I — Château du Clos Lucé, Amboise, France
  • Served as chief creative advisor to Francis I, producing designs for royal pageants, canal systems, and an ambitious planned city at Romorantin.
  • Continued anatomical studies and compiled notebooks on optics, geology, and hydrology.
  • Delivered St. John the Baptist and completed final revisions to St. Anne.
Painter & Military Engineer 1502 – 1506
Cesare Borgia / Republic of Florence / Charles d'Amboise — Central Italy
  • Mapped towns, fortifications, and river systems across Romagna for Cesare Borgia's military campaigns.
  • Commenced Mona Lisa and The Battle of Anghiari (Palazzo della Signoria mural).
  • Proposed and partially surveyed a canal to divert the Arno River around Pisa.
Court Artist & Engineer 1482 – 1499
Ludovico Sforza "il Moro" — Duchy of Milan
  • Designed festival sets, court pageants, and hydraulic systems for the Navigli canal network.
  • Produced The Virgin of the Rocks, Lady with an Ermine, and La Belle Ferronnière.
  • Completed The Last Supper (Santa Maria delle Grazie) — considered the defining work of narrative fresco composition.
  • Developed 200+ pages of designs for an equestrian bronze monument to Francesco Sforza; produced a 24-ft clay model destroyed in 1499.
  • Drafted early concepts for a solar-powered reflector, a helicopter-like aerial screw, and a tank.
Painter & Sculptor (Junior → Independent) 1466 – 1482
Bottega of Andrea del Verrocchio — Florence, Republic of Florence
  • Trained under Verrocchio in painting, sculpture, metalworking, and technical drawing.
  • Contributed the left-hand angel in Baptism of Christ; Verrocchio reportedly retired from painting upon seeing it.
  • Admitted to the Guild of Saint Luke (1472); accepted first independent commission, Adoration of the Magi (unfinished, 1481).
